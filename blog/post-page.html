Mastering the Maze:

A Handbook for filing the Z83 apllication form of the Republic of South Africa.

The Z83 form comes as a stamp for all employment openings in the Public sector of South Africa. Grasping the operation of the application form as well as the completion of it correctly can give you a superior probability of landing a government job. This article is intended to serve as a comprehensive guide which will elucidate all the vital aspects and the necessary points to be considered while following the process of filling the Z83 form.


Required Resources:
Z83 Application Form: The Department of Labour website(with the menu displayed below as the downloadable link).(n.d.). Step by Step guide to Z83 form filling to get employed in the Public Service Sector.
Black pen: make sure your writing is well-formatted, no grammar and spelling mistakes.
Patience and Accuracy: completing the form thoroughly and truthfully is vital.

Step-by-Step Guide

Gather information: Before you go on your application journey to Z83, do not forget to collect the necessary details. This refers to the job advertisement reference number(typically found in the advert).

SECTION A: Job Information(Straight forward): Here is the job reference number of the post and availability to start the post. Fill in "IMMEDIATELY" if you ready to begin straight upon selection, or put in the notice period you will need.

SECTION B: Provide name in full(As it appears on your ID), and ID/passport number. Accuracy is vital, double-check that your details are correct. Tick the appropriate boxes and honestly respond to the "Yes/No" question providing additional details on the questions which are asked in the elaboration part.

SECTION C: CONTACT DETAILS AND MEDIUM OF COMMUNICATION: Fill in the language you prefer for correspondence. Tick on method of correspondence preffered and fill in the contact details based on the method of correspondence you put in.

SECTION D: LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY: On the grey shaded part under the heading, fill in South African languages you know and state wether you are good, fair or poor in speaking writing or reading the language you filled in.

SECTION E: Qualifications: Put your formal qualifications down, beginining from the highest level of attainment down to the lowest. Hone the qualifications that are closely asking for by the position. Show the awarding institution, the fuculty of the major program of study for this qualification and the year when you graduated.

SECTION F: WORK EXPERIENCE: Arranging all your jobs in the chronological order starting with the most recent is desirable. Fill in your employer's name, the position you held, period and a reason why you left.

SECTION G: REFERENCES: Put your professional record contacts' details, at least three who are aware of your expertise and experience that, you show in the job application. Make sure their contact details are up to date.

DECLARATION: This part is of much importance. Cautiously scan the sheet, then put your signature and date it, making sure that it's done correctly and authentically.
